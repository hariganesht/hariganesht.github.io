I'm a senior undergraduate interested in the math behind human decision-making, and specifically how it changes under different circumstances and limitations.

I am currently a research intern at the AlphaGrep Quantitative Research Lab, working on modeling human cognition in financial markets. I am also fortunate to be an incoming IRIS Fellow guided by Prof Tan Zhi Xuan at the National University of Singapore, where I will study inefficient human decision-making and boundedly-rational agents.

Previously, I was a research intern on a collaborative project between IIT Madras and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, that aimed to develop methods for the early detection of Dementia in Alzheimer's patients.
